How To Use • Help & Tips

Start with General Weights (Projection / Injury / Age/Exp / Team Context), then use Position Multipliers to match your league. Use Advanced Adjustments to change the team-context recipe and age curves. Hover or tap ⓘ for quick slider explanations.

Quick Start
  1. Find your baseline: Don’t touch anything yet. Search a few players you care about and note where they sit.
  2. Set your philosophy:
    • Safer floor: increase Injury Risk, slightly decrease Projection.
    • Ceiling/upside: decrease Injury Risk, increase Projection, increase Age/Exp a little.
    • Situation matters: increase Team Context, then tune Advanced (by position).
  3. Match your league: Use Position Multipliers to reflect scoring/roster (ex: boost RB/WR for 2-flex leagues, reduce QB if 1QB).
  4. Spot-check: After any change, re-check 3–5 players at different positions to ensure the results “make sense”.
  5. Use the arrows: ▲ means the player moved up compared to baseline; ▼ means they fell.
How The Sliders Work

All sliders in this UI are offset sliders.

  • 0.00 means “use the built-in default.”
  • Positive offsets increase the default value.
  • Negative offsets decrease the default value.

Two key implications:

  • Even a “small” change can reshuffle ranks because the list is relative.
  • If you want to restore the original behavior, hit Reset (everything back to 0.00).
What Affects a Player’s Rank?

Each player’s final score is built from four normalized components:

  • Projection (how strong the expected production is)
  • Injury Risk (durability / injury history factor)
  • Age & Experience (age + experience curve by position)
  • Team Context (how favorable the player’s environment is)

Then the model applies:

  • Position Multiplier (global value adjustment by position)
  • Sample Reliability (less reliable samples get discounted)
  • QB Curve (optional QB compression so only elite QBs stay high in 1QB-style rankings)
General Weights [How To Use]
  • Projection: Higher = production dominates. Lower = other factors can “win” more often.
  • Injury Risk: Higher = durability matters a lot. Lower = you’re willing to take injury gambles.
  • Age/Exp: Higher = historical age/experience trends matter more. Lower = you care less about age curves.
  • Team Context: Higher = situation (pace, offense, QB play, etc.) matters more overall. Lower = situation matters less.

Rule of Thumb: If you’re surprised by movement, reduce the last slider you changed slightly (offsets stack quickly).

Postition Multipliers [How To Use]

Position multipliers are the fastest way to match your league format.

  • 1QB leagues: keep QB near baseline or even slightly lower.
  • Superflex / 2QB: increase QB multiplier noticeably.
  • 2+ flex leagues: RB/WR usually gain relative value.
  • TE premium: increase TE multiplier.

Note: If you change multipliers, expect your entire first ~50 picks to reshuffle — that’s normal.

Advanced Adjustments [How To Use]

Advanced Adjustments lets you change:

  • Age/Exp Curves by position (when a position “peaks” or falls off)
  • Team Context Recipe by position (what aspects of that team matter for each postion)

When should you use Advanced Adjustments?

  • If you already like your baseline ranks and only want minor tuning → stay in General Weights.
  • If you disagree with how team situation is being measured for a position → use Team Context Adjustments.
  • If you disagree with when a position peaks/declines → use Age/Exp Adjustments.
Common Presets / Draft Philosophies

These are conceptual presets (not exact numbers). Use small steps first.

  • Safe / High-Floor: Injury Risk ↑↑↑, Projection ↓, Team Context ↑↑
  • Upside / High-Ceiling: Injury Risk ↓↓↓, Projection ↑, Age/Exp ↑↑
  • Situation-Maxxing: Team Context ↑↑↑, then in Advanced Adjustments increase the signals you trust (ex: QB Play for WR/TE, O-line for RB).
  • Talent-over-situation: Team Context ↓↓, Projection ↑↑
Examples

Say you increase Team Context.

  • Players on fast, high-scoring offenses (and favorable role/team signals) tend to rise.
  • Players on slow, low-scoring offenses tend to fall.

Then if you also increase WR → QB Play in Advanced, WRs tied to strong QB situations rise even more.

Key idea: Team Context (General) controls “how much situation matters.” Advanced controls “what situation means.”

Troubleshooting / FAQ
  • Why did everyone move when I changed one slider? Rankings are relative — the list is re-sorted after every change.
  • My Team Context slider doesn’t do much: it may be too small of an offset, or your baseline already had context muted. Increase a bit more.
  • Advanced sliders feel too powerful: they directly change the recipe. Use tiny offsets and focus on 1–2 signals per position.
  • What does Reset do? Sets every offset back to 0.00 and clears movement arrows.
